Topic 11.4: Practice Scenario

A short branching exercise that puts you in the role of a Product Innovation Manager making real-time investment decisions — the same format you will encounter in Assessment 3

What this practice scenario is. The exercise below puts you in the role of a Product Innovation Manager who must make a sequence of investment decisions under time pressure. Each decision opens a different next decision, and the path you take depends on how you read the evidence in front of you. There is no single right answer — the discipline is the same one you have been building all week, applied at speed.

Why you are doing it now. Assessment 3 includes a similar branching exercise as part of the brief that releases on the Friday of Week 12. The exercise on this page is your low-stakes opportunity to experience the format, see how the timer works, and feel where the discipline of the translation move and the three claims becomes most useful. Nothing on this page is assessed.

How to use this practice scenario

  1. Have your three claims open. The drafted segment, scale, and key risk claims from Tutorial 11.3 are your reference material. The practice scenario will not test you on your specific concept, but the disciplines are the same.
  2. Read every decision page before you choose. The timer per decision is short. Reading first, deciding second is faster than reading and deciding together — the same is true in the assessed scenario.
  3. Notice where you hesitate. The moments where you are unsure are diagnostic. They tell you which of the three claims is least sharp in your own thinking. Make a note of these moments — they are where the most useful sharpening work for Week 12 will happen.
  4. Run the exercise more than once if you have time. Different paths will surface different reasoning. You will see different evidence in each path and your own decision pattern will become visible.

Practice Scenario — open below. Allow approximately 15 to 20 minutes for one full run. If the exercise does not load inside the page, open it directly at rmit-mktg1092.github.io/week11-practice-scenario/.

After you finish the practice scenario

Take three minutes and write down three things in your Assessment 3 working document. These notes are for you, not for submission, but they will sharpen your Week 12 work.

  1. The decision you felt most confident about. What made the confidence possible — was it the evidence in front of you, a framework you already knew, or a pattern you recognised from your group’s concept?
  2. The decision you felt least confident about. What was missing — specific evidence, a clearer framework, or a sharper sense of what the investor cared about?
  3. The pattern you noticed in your own reasoning. Did you default to optimistic readings of the evidence? Pessimistic? Risk-averse? The pattern is not a problem in itself, but knowing your own default helps you read your own Assessment 3 draft more honestly.

Tip. The practice scenario is not a quiz. There is no score at the end and no grade attached. The value is entirely in what you notice about your own reasoning under time pressure. Treat it the way a musician treats a sound check — the point is not the performance, it is hearing yourself clearly before the performance that matters.
